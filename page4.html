"Manila Bay is a toilet bowl that never gets flushed"
"If Boracay is a cesspool, then Manila Bay is a toilet bowl that’s used every day but never gets flushed,” said Antonio Oposo, an Environmental Lawyer. Oposa told the Senate committee on environment and natural resources that from the time he filed a case before the Supreme Court in 1999, the fecal coliform levels in the bay had increased 5 times. The current level far exceeds the safe level for swimming.

Emman Castillo, a tricycle driver who used to be a utility worker in Boracay said that "Kung marumi ang Boracay, ano pa kaya ang matatawag mo dito (If Boracay is dirty, then what do you call this)?” pertaining to the Manila Bay.
